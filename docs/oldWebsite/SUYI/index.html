Dataset Info:

The dataset I am visualizing is of demographic and academic student proxy data from Seattle Public Schools. The specific geo-zone can be seen here . The original dataset included over 1,200 rows of student data, but I chose to focus on k-8th grade students and thus filtered out grades 9-12. The demographic attributes include: grade, home language, ethnicity, school id federal race, country of origin. The academic data contains standardized state test scores and suspension/expulsion records.

Brief Description of Geo-Zone:
  • Academic Achievement Gap: Persistence of an academic achievement gap between students from different socio- economic backgrounds. In 2008-2009 there was a 40% difference in the WASL passing rate in Math and Science between low-income and middle/upper income students at Garfield High School.
  • Poverty: 36% of neighborhood residents live at or below the federal poverty level.
  • Demographic shifts: Significant demographic shifts challenge neighborhood residents to form community.

Motivation:

My motivation comes from my connection to this dataset. I went to the same high school as many of these students will end up going to. However, the educational outcomes and experiences displayed in the visualization differ from my own. This disparity is wrong at nature and leaves low-income students of color with limited life options. We can and should provide students with more opportunities and support. We can fulfill the promise of public education in this country, which is to provide all citizens with an equal chance to succeed.

Findings:

My visualization very quickly establishes the gap in educational outcomes between racial and socioeconomic groups. But more importantly the exploration should really begin to highlight outlying regions. Specifically does one racial group need more reading support because of English is not spoken inside the home? The gap is apparent but this is a tool for educators to find inconsistencies that must be fixed. The one thing that must be added is comparing across years.
